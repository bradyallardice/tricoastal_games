Tide of Avaris

Rival city-states. Shifting currents. The long game of trade across a sea that never sits still.

  • Players2–4
  • Time90–120 min
  • Ages12+
  • StatusIn Development

The pitch

Tide of Avaris is a medium-weight strategy game of seafaring trade and quiet warfare. Each player commands one of the great houses of the Avarine coast, sending fleets across a shared map of shifting currents to claim trade routes, occupy sacred islands, and outmaneuver the rival houses gathering at the same harbors.

The tide turns

The board changes between rounds. The tide is a shared resource and a shared obstacle — routes that were profitable last season may be closed this one, and a fleet that committed to a slow approach may suddenly find itself stranded or perfectly placed. Reading the tide is half the game.

A living map

Currents shift each round. Long-term plans have to account for a sea that doesn’t.

Trade as warfare

Direct conflict is rare and expensive. Most wars are fought by closing routes, undercutting markets, and stranding fleets.

Asymmetric houses

Each house plays differently — from the merchant princes of the southern coast to the storm-priests of the outer isles.

Tight footprint

A medium-weight game in a medium-weight box. Designed to fit on a kitchen table and finish in one sitting.
